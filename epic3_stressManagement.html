Everyday coping strategies

Reset your day

Simple ways to reduce stress fast

  • Pause for one minute and take slow deep breaths before starting your next task.
  • Break large assignments into smaller steps you can finish one at a time.
  • Use a timer for short work sessions, then take a brief mental reset.
  • Drink water, eat regularly, and avoid skipping sleep during busy weeks.
  • Step away from your screen for a quick walk, stretch, or quiet break.
Academic stress

When school work feels overwhelming

  • Write down your top three priorities for the day instead of trying to do everything at once.
  • Email your professor early if you are falling behind and need support.
  • Use a planner or calendar to map due dates before the week gets too busy.
  • Study in focused time blocks and silence distractions during each session.
  • Ask for tutoring, advising, or campus help before stress builds up.
Emotional balance

Healthy habits that support your mind

  • Journal what is stressing you out and identify one action you can take today.
  • Talk to a friend, mentor, counselor, coach, or advisor instead of carrying stress alone.
  • Limit doom-scrolling when you already feel mentally tired.
  • Give yourself realistic expectations instead of chasing perfection.
  • Celebrate progress, even if the day did not go exactly as planned.

Mindfulness practices

1 minute breathing reset

Try this between classes, before an exam, or anytime your mind feels overloaded.

Practice Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale for 4 seconds, and repeat 4 times.

5 senses grounding

Use this when you feel anxious, distracted, or mentally stuck.

Practice Name 5 things you see, 4 things you feel, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, and 1 thing you taste.

Mini reflection practice

This is helpful at the end of the day or after a stressful moment.

Practice Ask yourself: What am I feeling right now? What caused it? What is one healthy next step?
